Buyer's Guide

Weight Guidelines


Choosing the right weight is crucial for getting the most benefit from a weighted blanket. Generally, a blanket should weigh about 10% of your body weight for optimal comfort and effectiveness.

Material Types


Weighted blankets come in various materials, including cotton, minky, and organic fabrics. Cotton is breathable, while minky offers softness but may trap heat.

Cooling vs. Warmth


If you tend to sleep hot, look for breathable materials like cotton or those with cooling covers. Minky or heavier fabrics may retain heat, which could be uncomfortable.

Size Options


Ensure the size of the blanket fits your bed. Standard sizes include twin, full, queen, and king. A throw size may be suitable for targeted relief but won't cover an entire bed.

Price Range


Prices for weighted blankets can vary widely. Budget options may start around $40, while premium products can exceed $200. Consider investing in quality for long-term use.

Washability


Check if the blanket is machine washable or requires special care. Some blankets come with removable covers, making them easier to clean.

FAQ

What is the best weighted blanket for anxiety?


The best weighted blanket for anxiety varies by personal preference, but options like the Hand-Knitted Weighted Blanket from Silk & Snow and the Cotton Weighted Blanket from Bearaby are highly recommended for their comfort and breathability.

How much weight should I get for anxiety relief?


A good rule of thumb is to choose a blanket that weighs about 10% of your body weight. This amount provides adequate pressure without feeling overly restrictive.

Are weighted blankets safe for anxiety?


Yes, weighted blankets are generally safe and effective for anxiety relief. They provide deep pressure stimulation, which can help calm the nervous system.

What material is best for hot sleepers?


For hot sleepers, breathable materials like cotton or those with cooling features are ideal. Minky fabrics tend to trap heat, which may be uncomfortable.

Can weighted blankets be machine washed?


Many weighted blankets are machine washable, but always check the care instructions. Some may require special care or come with removable covers for easier cleaning.

What are the potential cons of weighted blankets?


Some potential cons include feeling too heavy for certain users, difficulty washing, and the possibility of overheating, especially with heavier or less breathable materials.
